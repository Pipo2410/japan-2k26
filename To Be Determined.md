## Camera on Jun 12 — Tokyo Tower walk + Roppongi nightlife

**Context:** Jun 12 currently combines the Tokyo Tower photo walk (Zojoji → Tokyo Tower → Akabanebashi 7-Eleven) with a Roppongi bar/club night. The Tokyo Tower walk is arguably the best camera moment of the trip (golden hour, lit tower, viral 7-Eleven shot). Bringing a camera into Roppongi clubs is risky — spilled drinks, theft, damage, plus the mental overhead of guarding it.

**Decision needed:** How to handle the camera that day.

### Option A (recommended) — Move the Tokyo Tower walk to Jun 11
- Jun 11 already ends with a "relaxed dinner in center" — no clubs
- Geography fits: Odaiba (teamLab, afternoon) → Yurikamome to Hamamatsucho/Shimbashi (~15 min) → Zojoji at golden hour → Tokyo Tower lit up → 7-Eleven shot → central dinner
- Makes Jun 11 the "big camera day" (teamLab + tower) and Jun 12 the worry-free bar night
- Trade-off: Jun 11 becomes a bit longer; may need to trim Joypolis or move faster through Odaiba

### Option B — Keep Jun 12, use a coin locker at Roppongi / Akabanebashi Station
- Japan's station coin lockers are reliable; camera-sized lockers run ~¥600–800
- Stash after the 7-Eleven shot, retrieve at 5am when first trains run (or whenever we leave the club)
- Risk: weekend Roppongi lockers can fill up — no guaranteed backup

### Option C — Detour to hotel between the photo walk and Roppongi
- Drop the camera at the hotel, then head to Roppongi
- From Ikebukuro (likely base) → Roppongi via hotel = ~50 min round-trip detour
- Eats into the evening, lowest-value option

**Leaning:** Option A — cleanest geographically, lets Jun 12 be a pure bar night.

## Camera strategy for heavy nightlife days (general)

---

### Five strategies for camera-heavy-nightlife days

#### 1. Shoot-and-drop (default for most days)
- Structure the day so all photo stops happen before dinner
- Return to the accommodation to drop the camera before heading out drinking
- **Best for:** days where we end up near the hotel anyway (Jun 7, Jun 10, Jun 13 — all Shinjuku/Ikebukuro-based)
- **Cost:** ~30–40 min detour; worth it for peace of mind

#### 2. Station coin locker
- Drop the camera at a major station locker near the nightlife district after the last photo stop
- Retrieve when first trains run (~5am) or whenever we leave
- **Best for:** days where going back to the hotel is a big detour (Jun 5 Kyoto sake crawl, Jun 12 Roppongi)
- **Risk:** weekend lockers fill up in Roppongi/Shibuya; not guaranteed; check beforehand

#### 3. Phone-only for the night
- Leave the camera at the hotel in the morning on days where the daytime camera value is low-to-medium
- Shoot everything on phone that day, accept lower photo quality
- **Best for:** days where daytime stops are street food / shopping / malls (May 28 Den Den Town, Jun 8 Akihabara, Jun 10 Ikebukuro)
- **Trade-off:** zero risk, zero quality

#### 4. Discreet crossbody + strict rules
- Bring the camera but in a small, unbranded crossbody bag (not a camera-branded backpack)
- Rules: never set it down, never open it in a club, stays on body at all times
- Skip venues with bag checks / no-camera policies (most real clubs in Shibuya/Roppongi will refuse entry with a camera anyway)
- **Best for:** bar hops with no real clubs (Jun 7 Golden Gai, May 29/31 izakaya hops)
- **Risk:** still vulnerable to spills, drunken knocks, pickpockets in dense crowds

#### 5. Designate a "camera day" with no nightlife
- Pair the biggest camera days with low-key evenings (ryokan nights, travel nights, relaxed dinners)
- Move remaining photo walks to earlier in the day on nightlife days
- Already applied in Option A above (Tokyo Tower walk moved to Jun 11, the teamLab + dinner day)
- **Best for:** pre-planning; prevents the conflict from existing in the first place
