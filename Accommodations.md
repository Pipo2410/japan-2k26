# Airbnb / apartment rental — verification checklist

Applies to any apartment booking across Tokyo, Osaka, Kyoto. The weak yen and shoulder-season timing means pricing is favorable — group apartments around €80/night (€16/person) are realistic in Namba/Nipponbashi right now. But Japan has specific regulations (Minpaku Law, 2018) that make listing quality vary. Verify before booking.

## Red flags to watch for

1. **License number** — legal Japan listings must display a Minpaku or hotel license number (looks like `M130000XXXX` or similar) somewhere in the listing. No number = illegal listing = real risk of cancellation or a crackdown mid-stay.
2. **"Sleeps 5" but only 1 bedroom** — common trick. Usually means 2 people in a double bed + 3 on futons on the living room floor. Fine if the group accepts it, just confirm before booking.
3. **Check-in method** — Japan requires ID verification + a check-in conversation. Legal hosts handle this via self-check-in with a lockbox + ID upload. Sketchy ones skip it — illegal, can mean eviction mid-stay.
4. **Minimum nights** — some Osaka/Kyoto neighborhoods enforce minpaku rules requiring 2+ night stays or banning weekend-only bookings.
5. **Cleaning fee hidden in total** — a €350 listing sometimes adds €80 cleaning at checkout. Filter by "total price" not "base price."
6. **Reviews with specific detail** — prefer listings with 10+ reviews. Read the 3-star ones (they tell the truth). "Small," "old building," "noisy street" are common honest downsides near Dotonbori / Shinjuku.

## What to verify before clicking book

- **Exact walking distance** to key landmarks (Dotonbori, Shinjuku station, Nishiki Market) — use Google Maps, don't trust the listing's claim
- **Floor and elevator** — a 5th-floor walk-up with 5 people + luggage is a pain
- **Nearest station + line** — ideally on a line that's on our transport plan for that city (Sakaisuji/Sennichimae/Midosuji in Osaka; Yamanote/Fukutoshin in Tokyo; Karasuma/Hankyu in Kyoto)
- **House rules** — some ban groups of 5 men (yes, really), early check-in, or noise after 22:00
- **Wi-Fi confirmed** and **washing machine** if we want to travel light
- **Message the host** before booking to confirm it sleeps 5 adults properly + confirm the license

## Platforms to cross-check

1. **Airbnb** — widest selection, best for unique apartments and Kyoto machiya
2. **Booking.com** — apartment-style hotels, often better cancellation policies
3. **Agoda** — typically 10–15% cheaper than Booking for the same Japanese property
4. **Rakuten Travel** — Japanese-native platform, surfaces listings foreign sites miss

Rule of thumb: if a listing looks great on Airbnb, check Booking/Agoda for the same property or building before committing.

# Tokyo

**Trip context:** 5 guys · 7 nights in Tokyo (Jun 7–14) · arriving from Hakone via Romancecar → Shinjuku · departing Narita or Haneda.

**Priorities:**
- Efficient access to itinerary spots (Shinjuku, Shibuya, Harajuku, Akihabara, Asakusa, Odaiba, Nakano, Mitaka/Ghibli, Roppongi)
- Reasonable price (skip Shibuya — too expensive)
- Group-friendly (bigger rooms / apartments)

---

## 1. Ikebukuro ⭐ Top Pick

**Why it wins:**
- **JR Yamanote loop** — the green loop line hitting Shinjuku (5 min), Shibuya (15 min), Harajuku, Akihabara (~15 min), Ueno. You'll live on this line.
- **Fukutoshin line** — direct to Shibuya + Harajuku without transfers (~10 min)
- **Narita Express stops here** — direct airport access, no Shinjuku transfer needed
- **20–30% cheaper** than Shinjuku/Shibuya for equivalent rooms
- **Group-friendly** — bigger rooms and apartment listings more available
- **Already on the itinerary (Jun 10)** — one "home night" is built in anyway
- **Marunouchi metro** for central Tokyo / Ginza access

**Downside:** A bit farther from Roppongi nightlife (Jun 12), but that's a taxi home regardless.

---

## 2. Shinjuku (specifically Shin-Okubo or Higashi-Shinjuku pockets)

**Why it's strong:**
- **Romancecar from Hakone drops you here** (Jun 7) — zero hassle on arrival day
- **4 itinerary days touch Shinjuku** — Jun 7, Jun 8 evening (8bit Café), Jun 10 transit, Jun 13 Kabukicho finale
- **Biggest transport hub on earth** — every line goes here
- **Shin-Okubo** (1 Yamanote stop north) or **Higashi-Shinjuku** = Shinjuku access at ~30% less cost. Korean town, lively, great cheap eats.

**Downside:** Central Shinjuku proper is expensive and chaotic. Only pick if a good group deal shows up.

---

## 3. Ueno

**Why consider:**
- **Keisei Skyliner** — 35 min direct to Narita, cheapest airport transfer
- On the Yamanote loop
- Walking distance to Asakusa (Jun 11)
- Cheaper than Shinjuku/Shibuya
- Good izakaya scene (Ameyoko market area)

**Downside:** Far from west-side nightlife — Shibuya/Roppongi are 25–30 min away. Most "fun" days are on the west side.

---

## 4. Nakano

**Why consider:**
- **Chuo line** — Shinjuku in 5 min, Mitaka/Ghibli in 10 min
- Local, cheap, authentic — not touristy
- Nakano Broadway on your doorstep (Jun 10 saved)

**Downside:** Smaller hotel selection, limited group apartment options, less nightlife immediately outside the door.

---

## 5. Asakusa (skip unless cheap)

- Cheapest area, traditional atmosphere
- **Too far east** — 40–50 min to Shibuya/Shinjuku where most nights happen
- Only worth it if a great deal on a 5-person Airbnb appears

---

## Recommendation

**Book Ikebukuro first.** Fall back to **Shin-Okubo** (Shinjuku-adjacent) if Ikebukuro pricing doesn't work out. Both give Yamanote access, both are cheaper than the glamour districts, and both have the space/apartment options that matter for 5 guys.

Shibuya is overrated as a base — you'll go there constantly regardless of where you sleep, so no reason to pay the premium for it.

---

## Tokyo booking checklist
- [ ] Search Ikebukuro — 5-person apartment or 2–3 rooms in same hotel
- [ ] Compare Shin-Okubo / Higashi-Shinjuku as backup
- [ ] Confirm walking distance to a Yamanote or Fukutoshin station (< 10 min)
- [ ] Check Narita Express / Skyliner access from chosen neighborhood

# Osaka

**Trip context:** 5 guys · 5 nights in Osaka (May 27 – Jun 1) · arriving KIX airport 14:30 · day trips to Nara and Kobe · departing via Shin-Osaka Shinkansen to Hiroshima.

**Priorities:**
- Efficient access to Dotonbori, Namba, Shinsaibashi, Amerikamura, Den Den Town, Shinsekai, Umeda
- Clean connections to Nara (Kintetsu/JR) and Kobe (Hankyu from Umeda)
- Reasonable price (skip central Shinsaibashi — premium zone)
- Group-friendly (bigger rooms / apartments)

Most evenings and nightlife are in the south (Minami). Base there.

---

## 1. Namba (Minami) ⭐ Top Pick

**Why it wins:**
- **Heart of the itinerary** — Dotonbori, Shinsaibashi, Amerikamura, Kuromon Ichiba all on foot
- **Multiple lines converge** — Midosuji (main artery), Sennichimae, Yotsubashi, Nankai, Kintetsu, JR
- **Nankai Rapi:t direct from KIX airport** (~38 min) — clean arrival
- **Kintetsu Namba → Nara** direct (May 30 trip sorted)
- **Midosuji line to Umeda** (~8 min) for Kobe day, and to **Shin-Osaka** (~15 min) for Hiroshima Shinkansen departure
- **Round One Namba** already on the itinerary — you'll be here daily anyway
- Food and izakayas everywhere when you stumble home

**Downside:** Central Namba hotels are mid-to-pricey. Fix this by going one stop off-center (see #2).

---

## 2. Nipponbashi / Den Den Town ⭐ Smart Value Pick

**Why it's the smart pick:**
- **One Sakaisuji stop east of Namba** (or 10 min walk) — everything Namba offers, 20–30% cheaper
- **Den Den Town on the doorstep** (May 28 saved)
- Walking distance to Dotonbori / Shinsaibashi
- Quieter at night = better sleep, Namba's chaos just minutes away
- Good group apartment availability (less tourist demand than Namba proper)

**Downside:** Slightly less "alive" immediately around the station.

---

## 3. Shinsaibashi

**Why consider:**
- North end of the Namba/Dotonbori strip, Midosuji line
- Shopping + restaurants everywhere
- Walking to all southern nightlife
- Slightly calmer than Namba core

**Downside:** Pricing close to Namba with less upside.

---

## 4. Tennoji

**Why consider:**
- **Southern JR Loop hub** — cheap, local, good connectivity
- Walking / short ride to Shinsekai (May 29) and Tsutenkaku
- **Direct JR to Nara** (alternative to Kintetsu)
- Abeno Harukas tower + strong budget hotel supply
- Airport access via JR Haruka

**Downside:** 10 min subway to Namba — not on foot, so every night out costs a ride.

---

## 5. Kitahama (known quantity — Best Western Plus Fino option)

**Context:** We stayed at **Best Western Plus Hotel Fino Osaka Kitahama** on a previous trip. Worth evaluating as a returning option.

**Why consider:**
- **Quiet business/finance district** — calm at night, good sleep, cheaper than Namba/Shinsaibashi
- **Sakaisuji line direct** — Kitahama → Nippombashi (Den Den Town) in ~5 min → short walk to Dotonbori
- **5 min walk to Yodoyabashi** for Midosuji line access → Umeda (3 min) and Namba (7 min)
- **Nakanoshima island + riverside** on the doorstep — nice morning walks
- **Walkable to Osaka Castle** (~20 min, or 1 subway stop)
- **Best Western Plus Fino specifically** — reliable mid-range chain, clean, decent value
- **Familiarity** — we already know the hotel and area; zero unknowns for a group trip

**Downside:**
- **Every night is Minami-based** (Dotonbori, Namba, Amerikamura) — expect a subway commute back each evening; last train on Sakaisuji/Midosuji is ~midnight, taxi after that
- **Dead around the hotel at night** — office district, restaurants close early, no stumble-home vibe
- **Group sizing problem** — Best Western rooms are typically twins/doubles, so 5 guys = 3 rooms minimum → often ends up pricier than a single 5-person apartment in Nipponbashi/Namba
- **No walk-home nightlife** — Nipponbashi/Namba lets you roll from a bar to bed in 5 min; Kitahama = subway or taxi

**Verdict:** Solid if the price is materially better than a Nipponbashi/Namba 5-person apartment. Pick Minami if pricing is similar — saves 30–40 min of transit per day and the "walk home drunk" factor is non-trivial.

---

## 6. Umeda (Kita)

**Why consider:**
- **Shin-Osaka Shinkansen** 1 stop away (Jun 1 departure easy)
- **Hankyu direct to Kobe** (May 31 Kobe day)
- Osaka Castle closer from here
- Business-district polish

**Downside:** **Far from the nightlife that dominates every evening.** You'd be riding the Midosuji line back and forth constantly. Wrong side of the city for this itinerary.

---

## 7. Shin-Imamiya (budget warning)

- Cheapest beds in Osaka (Nishinari/Airin backpacker area)
- JR Loop line + Nankai access, walking to Shinsekai
- **Rough reputation in parts** — historically Osaka's skid-row area. Fine by day, some streets sketchy at night. Not a dealbreaker for 5 guys but worth knowing.

---

## 8. Shin-Osaka (skip)

Business district, dead at night, wrong side for nightlife. Only makes sense if arriving/departing same-day.

---

## Osaka recommendation

**Book Nipponbashi or Namba south-edge** (around Namba station but outside the Shinsaibashi premium zone). Everything on foot, Nara/Kobe/airport/Shinkansen trains all work cleanly, and 20–30% savings vs. central Namba. **Tennoji** is the fallback if nothing good shows up.

---

## Osaka budget reality (5 nights, May 27 – Jun 1)

**Realistic target:** under **€400 total for the group** = ~€80/night = **€16/person/night**. Confirmed achievable on Airbnb for 2-bedroom apartments in Nipponbashi / Namba south-edge right now (weak yen + shoulder-season timing helps).

| Tier | Example | 5-night total for 5 guys |
|------|---------|--------------------------|
| **Budget sweet spot** | Airbnb 2BR, Nipponbashi / outer Namba | **€350–500** |
| Mid | Nicer Airbnb, Namba south-edge | €600–900 |
| Hotel split | 3× twin rooms at APA / Toyoko / Fino Kitahama | €700–1,000 |
| Premium | MIMARU Shinsaibashi | ~€1,500 |

Weekend effect: Fri May 29 + Sat May 30 nights will run 20–30% above weekday average — factor this in.

**Move:** target an Airbnb in the **€70–100/night** range in Nipponbashi. At that price, beats Best Western Plus Fino on total cost while staying walking distance to Dotonbori.

## Osaka booking checklist
- [ ] Search Airbnb for "entire place, 5 guests" in Nipponbashi / Namba south-edge, filter by total price under €500
- [ ] Run every shortlisted listing through the [verification checklist](#airbnb--apartment-rental--verification-checklist) above
- [ ] Confirm walking distance to Midosuji or Sakaisuji station (< 10 min)
- [ ] Check access to Kintetsu Namba (Nara) and Midosuji line (Umeda/Shin-Osaka)
- [ ] Message host to confirm sleeps 5 adults properly + ask for license number
- [ ] Cross-check the same area on Booking.com / Agoda for apartment-hotel alternatives
- [ ] Compare against Best Western Plus Fino Kitahama (known quantity) total price before committing
- [ ] Budget backup: Tennoji area if Nipponbashi inventory is thin

# Kyoto

**Trip context:** 5 guys · 3 nights in Kyoto (Jun 3 – Jun 6) · arriving by Shinkansen from Hiroshima (Kyoto Station) · departing by Shinkansen to Odawara/Hakone (Kyoto Station).

**Priorities:**
- Efficient access to Nishiki Market, Pontocho, Gion, Fushimi Inari, Higashiyama temples, Arashiyama, Kinkaku-ji
- Reasonable price (Kyoto runs pricier than Osaka overall — machiya house rentals often win for groups)
- Group-friendly

**Key Kyoto quirk:** the subway is only 2 lines and many sights (Kinkaku-ji, Ginkaku-ji, Philosopher's Path) are **bus-only**. Proximity to a major bus hub matters as much as subway access. A **central downtown base** beats a station base here.

---

## 1. Downtown Kyoto — Kawaramachi / Shijo ⭐ Top Pick

**Why it wins:**
- **Walking to Nishiki Market, Pontocho Alley, Gion** — three itinerary spots on foot
- **Keihan line → Fushimi Inari** direct (Jun 3 dusk visit)
- **Hankyu Kawaramachi** direct to Arashiyama area (Jun 5, transfer at Katsura)
- **Main city bus hub** — you'll need buses for Kinkaku-ji, Ginkaku-ji, Philosopher's Path
- Best restaurant/izakaya density in the city — sake bar crawl (Jun 5 night) is on your doorstep
- 15 min from Kyoto Station by subway/bus for Shinkansen in (Jun 3) and out (Jun 6)

**Downside:** Mid-to-premium pricing, central tourist zone.

---

## 2. Karasuma / Shijo-Karasuma ⭐ Smart Value Pick

**Why it's the smart pick:**
- **5 min walk west of Kawaramachi** — same downtown access, lower prices
- **Karasuma subway line** direct to Kyoto Station (Shinkansen in/out)
- **Hankyu Karasuma** for Arashiyama direction
- Walking to Nishiki Market
- Quieter at night, less tourist-packed than Kawaramachi proper
- Good machiya (traditional townhouse) rental density — ideal for 5 guys in one house

**Downside:** Slightly less atmosphere than being right on Pontocho.

---

## 3. Kyoto Station Area

**Why consider:**
- **Shinkansen on your doorstep** — cleanest arrival + departure (Jun 3 + Jun 6)
- Biggest hotel supply in the city — best chance of finding 5-person deals
- Bus terminal at the station = every neighborhood reachable
- Cheaper than downtown
- Good for early Arashiyama departure (Jun 5) via JR Sagano line

**Downside:** **No atmosphere.** Big station district, dead at night, you'll commute 15–20 min every evening to where the fun is.

---

## 4. Gion / Higashiyama

**Why consider:**
- **Most atmospheric neighborhood in Japan** — ryokan, lantern-lit streets
- Walking to Kiyomizu-dera, Sannen-zaka, Gion, Philosopher's Path (Jun 4 all on foot)
- Keihan line for Fushimi Inari

**Downside:** **Premium pricing.** Ryokan-style stays are expensive for 3 nights × 5 guys. Farther from Nishiki/Pontocho nightlife (20 min walk or short ride). Worth it if one night is a splurge ryokan; overkill for the full stay.

---

## 5. Nijo / Oike

**Why consider:**
- Central but quieter, near Nijo Castle
- **Tozai subway line** — east to Higashiyama, west to near Arashiyama (Uzumasa Tenjingawa)
- Walking to Nishiki Market (10–15 min)
- Slightly cheaper than Kawaramachi

**Downside:** Less immediate food/bar density than downtown.

---

## 6. Arashiyama (skip)

Gorgeous but **far west, tourist-commuter area.** Dies after dark, 30+ min commute to everything else. Visit, don't sleep here.

---

## Kyoto recommendation

**Book Shijo-Karasuma area** — it's the sweet spot: downtown walkability at 15–20% below Kawaramachi pricing, subway direct to Kyoto Station, strong machiya rental supply. Fall back to **Kawaramachi proper** if you want the absolute best location. **Kyoto Station area** only if budget forces it.

**Group-of-5 tip:** Kyoto has excellent **machiya (traditional townhouse) rentals** — entire wooden houses with tatami rooms, private courtyards, often cheaper per-person than 2–3 hotel rooms. Search Airbnb/Booking for "machiya" in the Shijo-Karasuma or Nakagyo ward area. This is often the best-value option for a group here.

---

## Kyoto booking checklist
- [ ] Search Shijo-Karasuma / Nakagyo ward — prioritize machiya house rental for 5
- [ ] Compare Kawaramachi as atmosphere upgrade
- [ ] Confirm walking distance to Nishiki Market or a bus hub (< 10 min)
- [ ] Check Keihan line access (Fushimi Inari) and Karasuma subway (Kyoto Station)
- [ ] Optional: book 1 splurge night at a Higashiyama ryokan if budget allows

# Hiroshima

**Trip context:** 5 guys · 2 nights (Jun 1 – Jun 3) · arriving by Shinkansen from Shin-Osaka · departing by Shinkansen to Kyoto · core activities are Peace Memorial Park and the Miyajima Island day trip.

**Priorities:**
- Shinkansen in/out convenience (only 2 nights — logistics matter)
- Fast access to Peace Park (Jun 1 afternoon) and Miyajimaguchi ferry (Jun 2)
- Reasonable price
- Group-friendly

**Key quirk:** Hiroshima is compact. Trams (not subway) are the main transport. Everything is 15–20 min from everywhere, so the trade-off is mostly **station convenience vs. city-center atmosphere.**

---

## 1. Hiroshima Station Area ⭐ Top Pick (for 2 nights)

**Why it wins:**
- **Shinkansen on your doorstep** — clean arrival Jun 1, clean departure Jun 3
- **JR Sanyo line direct to Miyajimaguchi** (Jun 2 ferry, ~25 min)
- **Tram hub** — direct line to Peace Park (~15 min) and to the Atomic Bomb Dome
- Largest hotel supply in the city → best chance of good group deals
- Newly redeveloped station area — good restaurants, okonomiyaki options on-site (Jun 1 dinner easy)
- With only 2 nights and zero nightlife on the itinerary, station convenience wins

**Downside:** Less atmosphere than central Hiroshima (but you'll be out sightseeing anyway).

---

## 2. Kamiyacho / Hatchobori (City Center)

**Why consider:**
- **Closest to Peace Memorial Park** — walking distance (~10 min)
- **Hondori covered shopping arcade** — okonomiyaki restaurants, bars, shops
- Tram hub — direct lines to station and Miyajimaguchi
- More atmospheric than the station area
- Best okonomiyaki spots are here (Okonomimura building)

**Downside:** Hauling luggage in/out to/from Shinkansen takes a tram ride each way. Minor for 2 nights but worth noting.

---

## 3. Peace Park / Nakajima-cho

**Why consider:**
- **Right by the Peace Memorial** — contemplative setting
- Quiet at night, riverside walks
- Walking to the A-Bomb Dome

**Downside:** Fewer hotels, fewer group-friendly options, quiet = fewer dinner options nearby.

---

## 4. Miyajima Island (skip for base)

Staying on Miyajima is magical (deer on your doorstep, torii at sunrise), but **impractical for this itinerary** — you'd drag luggage across the ferry twice, and you'd lose easy access to the Peace Park / Shinkansen. Reserve this as a "next trip" idea.

---

## Hiroshima recommendation

**Book Hiroshima Station area.** With only 2 nights, Shinkansen-adjacent + JR-direct-to-Miyajimaguchi beats everything else. Fall back to **Kamiyacho** if you want to be in the middle of the okonomiyaki scene and don't mind the tram ride with luggage.

---

## Hiroshima booking checklist
- [ ] Search Hiroshima Station area (north or south exit) — 5-person apartment or 2–3 rooms
- [ ] Compare Kamiyacho / Hatchobori if atmosphere matters more than logistics
- [ ] Confirm walking distance to station or tram stop (< 10 min)
- [ ] Verify JR line access for Miyajimaguchi ferry

# Hakone

**Trip context:** 5 guys · 1 night (Jun 6 – Jun 7) · arriving by Shinkansen Kyoto → Odawara, then local train/bus into Hakone · departing via Romancecar to Shinjuku · this is the **ryokan + onsen + kaiseki dinner night** — the experience, not just a bed.

**Priorities:**
- Authentic ryokan with onsen (ideally outdoor / rotenburo) and kaiseki dinner
- Accommodation that fits 5 guys (check if room types allow groups — some ryokan cap at 2–4)
- Reasonable access from Odawara (arrival) and to Hakone-Yumoto (Romancecar departure)
- Scenic, quiet setting — this is the "switch off" night of the trip

**Key difference from other cities:** you're not picking a *neighborhood*, you're picking a *ryokan and its area of Hakone*. The area determines vibe + price + access.

---

## 1. Hakone-Yumoto ⭐ Top Pick (value + convenience)

**Why it wins:**
- **Entry gateway to Hakone** — direct Romancecar terminus for Jun 7 departure (no extra transfers with luggage)
- **15 min by local train from Odawara** (Jun 6 arrival)
- **Highest density of ryokan** — best chance of finding one that takes groups of 5
- Best value tier — Yumoto is the most mainstream area, so pricing is most competitive
- Still has proper onsen town vibe (hot spring river, yukata strolls, steaming streets)

**Downside:** The most touristed area of Hakone. Not the most remote or scenic option.

---

## 2. Gora (scenic upgrade)

**Why consider:**
- **Higher elevation, mountain views** — forested, more dramatic than Yumoto
- Access to the Hakone Open-Air Museum (famous outdoor sculpture park)
- Many upscale ryokan with private outdoor onsen (rotenburo)
- Served by the Hakone Tozan line from Yumoto + the cable car up the mountain

**Downside:** More transfers on arrival + departure with luggage. Pricier than Yumoto on average.

---

## 3. Miyanoshita (historic)

**Why consider:**
- Historic onsen town — home of the legendary **Fujiya Hotel** (1878, classic Meiji-era architecture)
- Midway between Yumoto and Gora on the Tozan line
- Mix of traditional ryokan and Western historic hotels
- Quieter than Yumoto, more accessible than Gora

**Downside:** Smaller selection, some places skew old-money formal.

---

## 4. Sengokuhara (luxury retreat)

**Why consider:**
- **Pampas grass fields + remote mountain setting** — most peaceful area of Hakone
- Luxury ryokan territory — serious onsen experiences
- Clear Mt. Fuji views on good days

**Downside:** **Furthest from Yumoto** — bus or transfers required on arrival and departure. Often the priciest tier. For 1 night with luggage, logistics are a pain.

---

## 5. Lake Ashi / Moto-Hakone (skip for sleeping)

Beautiful for the torii-on-water shot and the pirate ship cruise, but **awkward for an overnight** — poor connections to Yumoto/Odawara, not many ryokan. Visit during the day, don't sleep here.

---

## Hakone recommendation

**Book a ryokan in Hakone-Yumoto.** For a single night with 5 people and luggage transitions on both sides, Yumoto's directness is worth more than Gora's scenery. Prioritize a ryokan with:

- **Rotenburo (outdoor onsen)** — non-negotiable for the experience
- **Kaiseki dinner included** — multi-course traditional meal, the other half of the experience
- **Rooms that actually take groups of 5** — many ryokan max at 2–4 people per room; you may need 2 adjacent tatami rooms
- **Tattoo policy** (if anyone in the group has tattoos) — some traditional onsen still refuse entry; check or look for private/in-room baths (kashikiri)

Upgrade to **Gora** if budget allows and someone is willing to handle the extra transfers for the scenery payoff.

---

## Hakone booking checklist
- [ ] Pick area: Hakone-Yumoto (default) or Gora (scenic upgrade)
- [ ] Confirm ryokan takes groups of 5 (ask explicitly — email in advance)
- [ ] Confirm kaiseki dinner + breakfast included
- [ ] Confirm rotenburo (outdoor onsen) on-site
- [ ] Check tattoo policy / availability of private baths (kashikiri)
- [ ] Book early — quality Hakone ryokan fill up, especially weekends (Jun 6 is a Saturday)
