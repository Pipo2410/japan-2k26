# Japan Trip — Research

## OSAKA
**Base: ~5 nights**

Highlights:
- Dotonbori — neon strip, street food (takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu), nightlife
- Osaka Castle + surrounding park
- Shinsekai — old-school working class Osaka, great food, retro vibe
- Kuromon Ichiba Market — fresh food market, great for snacking
- Amerikamura ("Amemura") — youth culture, streetwear, bars
- Den Den Town — Osaka's anime/manga/retro gaming district (like a smaller Akihabara)
- Namba — main entertainment district

Gaming:
- Round One Namba — massive arcade, open 24h, bowling, sports, crane games, rhythm games

Day trips:
- Nara (45 min): deer park, Todai-ji (giant Buddha), Kasuga Taisha shrine
- Kobe (30 min): Kobe beef, Kitano-cho Western houses, harbor

Food:
- Takoyaki (octopus balls) — Osaka specialty
- Okonomiyaki — savory pancake
- Kushikatsu — deep-fried skewers (Shinsekai)
- Ramen — many regional styles

## KYOTO
**3 nights**

Highlights:
- Fushimi Inari — thousands of torii gates up a mountain, best at dawn or dusk, free
- Arashiyama — bamboo grove, Tenryu-ji garden, monkey park, river, rickshaws
- Gion district — geisha district, atmospheric evening walks, ochaya teahouses
- Kinkaku-ji — Golden Pavilion (very crowded, go early)
- Nishiki Market — narrow covered market, food stalls, pickles, street snacks
- Philosopher's Path — canal walk lined with cherry trees (past bloom in June but still nice)
- Pontocho Alley — narrow lantern-lit alley of bars and restaurants, great for dinner

Anime/manga:
- K-Books Kyoto — anime/manga/doujin merch

## HIROSHIMA
**2 nights**

Highlights:
- Peace Memorial Museum — one of the most powerful museums in the world, emotionally heavy
- Atomic Bomb Dome (Genbaku Dome) — preserved ruin, UNESCO site, adjacent to the museum
- Peace Memorial Park — walk around, reflection

Day 2 — Miyajima Island (30 min by train + ferry):
- Itsukushima Shrine — "floating" torii gate in the sea (iconic)
- Wild deer roam freely on the island
- Mt. Misen hike (ropeway available) — views over the Seto Inland Sea
- Fresh oysters — Hiroshima is famous for them

Food:
- Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki — layered with noodles, different from Osaka style
- Oysters — grilled, raw, fried

Transport: Shinkansen from Shin-Osaka ~1.5h, ~€61/person. Onward to Kyoto ~1.5h, ~€68/person (same Sanyo/Tokaido line, passes through Shin-Osaka)

## HAKONE
**1 night**

Why stay: Ryokan experience — traditional Japanese inn, outdoor onsen (hot spring), multi-course kaiseki dinner, Mt. Fuji views (weather permitting in June)
- Mt. Fuji views often obscured in June (rainy season) — manage expectations
- Hakone Open Air Museum — sculpture park, worth a morning visit
- Romancecar train from Shinjuku (scenic, book in advance)
- Or: Shinkansen to Odawara → local train/bus into Hakone

Cost: ~€100-120/person incl. dinner + breakfast at a mid-range ryokan
Book well in advance — popular and limited capacity

Transport: Kyoto → Odawara by Shinkansen (~2h, ~€81/person), then local train/bus into Hakone

## TOKYO
**7 nights**

### Neighborhoods

**Akihabara** — Electric Town. Anime, manga, figures, retro games, maid cafes, electronics. Dense and overwhelming in the best way.

**Nakano Broadway** — Less touristy than Akihabara. 4-floor mall of otaku shops, retro game stores, rare figures. Preferred by locals.

**Ikebukuro** — Animate headquarters (largest anime store in Japan), Sunshine City mall, Pokemon Center, another gaming hub. Also good nightlife.

**Shinjuku** — Everything:
- Golden Gai: ~200 tiny themed bars in an alleyway, each fits 6-8 people, incredible atmosphere
- Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane): narrow alley of yakitori stalls, smoky, old Tokyo
- Kabukicho: entertainment district, host clubs, izakayas, nightlife, "Robot Restaurant" area
- Clubs: nightlife concentrated here

**Shibuya** — Iconic scramble crossing, shopping, younger nightlife scene
- Round One Shibuya — multiple floors, massive
- Clubs: Sound Museum Vision, Womb (legendary techno club)

**Harajuku/Omotesando** — Takeshita Street (cosplay, youth fashion), Omotesando (upscale)

**Asakusa** — Old Tokyo feel, Senso-ji temple, Nakamise shopping street, rickshaws

**Odaiba** — Futuristic man-made island:
- Life-size Gundam statue
- teamLab Borderless (immersive digital art — book in advance, very popular)
- Joypolis — indoor Sega theme park

**Roppongi** — International nightlife, expat bars, clubs

**Shimokitazawa/Koenji** — Indie/underground culture, vintage shops, live music venues

### Gaming
- Round One Shibuya (massive, multiple floors)
- Super Potato (Akihabara) — retro game shop, legendary
- Taito and Sega game centers throughout the city
- Nakano Broadway for rare/retro finds

### Nightlife
- Golden Gai (Shinjuku) — must do
- Kabukicho bar hopping
- Karaoke: Big Echo, Karaoke Kan (many locations)
- Clubs: Womb, Sound Museum Vision (Shibuya), Contact, UNIT
- Host clubs (Kabukicho) — if curious
- Izakayas everywhere — the default social eating/drinking format

### Food highlights
- Ramen: Ichiran (solo booths), Fuunji (tsukemen), many regional shops
- Sushi: Tsukiji outer market (still active for food stalls), conveyor belt sushi (kaiten)
- Wagyu beef — splurge meal option
- Yakitori alleys
- Convenience store food (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart) — genuinely great, not a joke

## PRACTICAL INFO

### Transport
- IC Card (Suica/Pasmo) — load it up, use for all local trains, buses, even convenience stores
- Shinkansen tickets: buy at station or online (Klook, JR Kyushu website, etc.)
- No JR Pass needed for this route
- Google Maps works well for transit navigation in Japan

### Money
- Japan is still heavily cash-based — carry yen
- ATMs at 7-Eleven and Japan Post always accept foreign cards
- Most restaurants and izakayas cash only

### Language
- English signage is good in tourist areas and train stations
- Translation apps (Google Translate camera mode) very useful for menus
- Most young people have basic English

### Etiquette
- No eating/drinking while walking (except festival stalls)
- Quiet on trains, no phone calls
- Cash tips are not done in Japan — considered rude
- Onsen rules: full nudity, no tattoos in many traditional ones (Hakone ryokan — check their policy)

### Bookings to do in advance
- Hakone ryokan (limited rooms, very popular)
- teamLab Borderless Tokyo
- Popular ramen shops sometimes have queues — just queue

## CAR / DRIFT CULTURE (user interested — to revisit later)

### Daikoku Futo PA (top priority)
- Highway rest area in Yokohama, legendary JDM / tuner / supercar meet spot
- Best nights: Friday + Saturday from ~10pm
- Cannot be accessed on foot — must enter from the highway (Bayshore Route)
- Fits perfectly into Jun 13 (Sat) evening in Tokyo, pairs with Kabukicho afterward
- Access options:
  - Guided "Daikoku car meet tour" (GoWithGuide, Viator, Japan Car Tours) — ~€80-120/person, driver brings group in a JDM car
  - Private driver charter
  - Self-drive with International Driving Permit
- Weather note: June is rainy season, attendance drops if raining

### Backup car meet spots
- Tatsumi PA — Tokyo Bay side, similar vibe
- Umihotaru PA — Tokyo Bay, scenic, more mixed crowd

### Hakone Turnpike (Mazda Turnpike)
- Legendary touge road referenced in Initial D lore
- Mountain curves with Mt. Fuji views
- Perfectly positioned: they're already in Hakone Jun 6-7
- Option: rent a car in Odawara for half-day, drive the turnpike on Jun 6 (Sat) arrival afternoon or Jun 7 (Sun) morning

### Car-related shopping (Tokyo)
- Super Autobacs Tokyo Bay (Shin-Kiba) — biggest tuning/parts superstore in Japan
- Up Garage — used JDM parts (multiple Tokyo locations)
- Nismo Omori Factory — Nissan tuning showroom + shop

### Drifting (far, pilgrimage-level)
- Ebisu Circuit (Fukushima) — drift mecca, home of Drift Matsuri
- ~3-4h north of Tokyo, realistically a separate side trip
- Some tour companies run drift taxi experiences closer to Tokyo
