# Japan Trip — Decisions

## Route: Structure A chosen (revised order)
**Osaka (5n) → Hiroshima (2n) → Kyoto (3n) → Hakone (1n) → Tokyo (7n)**

Options considered:
- **A (chosen):** Natural west-to-east sweep, proper time everywhere
- **B:** Trim Hiroshima to 1 night, gain a Tokyo night — rejected because Miyajima alone deserves half a day, cramming it is a waste
- **C:** Add Fukuoka (2n) — rejected because it's further west (more backtracking), reduces Kyoto and Tokyo time, adds complexity

## Order revised: Hiroshima before Kyoto
Initial plan was Osaka → Kyoto → Hiroshima → Hakone → Tokyo. Flipped to Osaka → Hiroshima → Kyoto → Hakone → Tokyo because:
- Shinkansen line runs west-to-east: Hiroshima → Shin-Osaka → Kyoto → Nagoya → Tokyo
- Going Kyoto→Hiroshima→Tokyo means backtracking west through Osaka, then a long 760km haul east from Hiroshima to Hakone
- Going Hiroshima→Kyoto→Hakone is a clean linear east sweep, Kyoto→Odawara is only ~450km vs Hiroshima→Odawara ~760km
- Total Shinkansen cost roughly equivalent (~€201 vs ~€210 per person), but the flow is much better and less tiring on the long eastbound leg

## Hiroshima: 2 nights (not 1, not a day trip)
The must-do combo is Peace Memorial Museum + Miyajima Island (floating torii gate). Miyajima alone is a half-day. Doing both properly requires 2 nights. 1 night is possible but rushed.

## Kyoto: 3 nights (not 2, not a day trip from Osaka)
Kyoto has too much to cover as a day trip — Fushimi Inari, Arashiyama bamboo grove, Gion district, Kinkaku-ji, Nishiki Market, Philosopher's Path. 3 nights allows a relaxed pace and an evening atmosphere that day trippers miss entirely.

## Hakone: 1 night (not a day trip)
The reason to go to Hakone is the ryokan experience — traditional Japanese inn, outdoor onsen, multi-course kaiseki dinner. As a day trip you skip all of that. 1 night is the right call. Fits naturally on the Kansai→Tokyo route.

## No JR Pass
Individual Shinkansen tickets for this route are cheaper than any JR Pass option. Main intercity legs: Osaka-Hiroshima (~€37), Hiroshima-Odawara (~€125), Odawara-Tokyo (short). No need for 7/14/21-day pass.

## Osaka as starting hub (not Tokyo)
Kansai region (Kyoto, Nara, Hiroshima, Kobe) is all close to Osaka. Doing this first while energy is high makes sense. Tokyo at the end is easier to enjoy when tired — you can wander neighborhoods without planning day trips.

## Nara: day trip from Osaka (no overnight)
Nara is 45 min from Osaka, 1 hour from Kyoto. Deer park + Todai-ji is a half-day to full day. No reason to stay overnight.

## Kobe: half-day from Osaka (no overnight)
Kobe beef lunch, Kitano-cho area, harbor. Easy half-day. No overnight needed.

## No Universal Studios Japan (USJ)
Do NOT plan USJ / Nintendo World into the Osaka itinerary. The group is not interested despite the anime/gaming angle. Osaka days are city exploration instead (Castle, Umeda, Shinsekai, Den Den Town, Round One, Dotonbori, Amerikamura). Re-confirmed twice — do not re-suggest in future sessions.

## Shinkansen costs corrected (not ~€37 for Osaka-Hiroshima)
Earlier ~€37 figure was wrong. Accurate Shinkansen legs per person:
- Shin-Osaka → Hiroshima: ~€61
- Hiroshima → Kyoto: ~€68
- Kyoto → Odawara: ~€81
- Odawara → Tokyo: ~€15
Total route Shinkansen: ~€225/person
