## Osaka — May 27–June 1

### May 27 (Wed) · Arrival
- Land 14:30, get IC cards (Suica/Pasmo) at airport, check in
- Evening: Dotonbori — first street food (takoyaki, okonomiyaki), neon walk
- Easy night, recover from flight

### May 28 (Thu) · Osaka City — North
- Morning: Osaka Castle + grounds
- Afternoon: Umeda area — Umeda Sky Building (rooftop floating garden, great views), Hep Five ferris wheel
- Evening: Den Den Town (anime, manga, retro games, figures)
- Night: Round One Namba — arcade, bowling, sports (open 24h)

### May 29 (Fri) · Osaka City — South
- Morning: Shinsekai (old-school Osaka, kushikatsu lunch), Tsutenkaku Tower area
- Afternoon: Shinsaibashi shopping street, Dotonbori
- Evening: Amerikamura — bars, street culture
- Night: izakaya bar hop, Namba nightlife

### May 30 (Sat) · Nara day trip
- 45 min by train from Osaka Namba
- Deer Park — deer roam free, can feed them
- Todai-ji Temple — giant bronze Buddha, one of the largest wooden buildings in the world
- Kasuga Taisha Shrine — lantern-lined path through forest
- Back to Osaka for evening — Amerikamura (bars, street culture)

### May 31 (Sun) · Kobe + Dotonbori
- Morning: Kobe by train (30 min) — Kitano-cho Western houses, harbor walk
- Lunch: Kobe beef — splurge, this is the place to do it
- Afternoon back in Osaka: Kuromon Ichiba Market (fresh food, snacks), Dotonbori area
- Evening: Namba nightlife — izakaya bar hop

### Jun 1 (Mon) · Travel to Hiroshima
- Morning: check out, Shinkansen from Shin-Osaka to Hiroshima (~1.5h, ~€61/person)
- Afternoon: check in, Peace Memorial Park + Atomic Bomb Dome

## Kyoto — June 3–6

### Jun 3 (Wed) · Arrival + Fushimi Inari
- Arrive Kyoto from Hiroshima by Shinkansen (morning), check in
- Afternoon: Nishiki Market (covered food market, snacks and pickles)
- Evening: Fushimi Inari — thousands of torii gates up the mountain, magical at dusk
  - Hike up as far as you want (full loop is 2–3h; first viewpoint is ~30 min)
- Dinner: Pontocho Alley — narrow lantern-lit alley of bars and restaurants

### Jun 4 (Thu) · East Kyoto · Higashiyama + Gion
- Morning: Kiyomizu-dera temple — iconic wooden stage, views over Kyoto
- Walk down Sannen-zaka + Ninen-zaka — preserved stone streets, matcha and souvenir shops
- Afternoon: Philosopher's Path (2km canal walk) → Ginkaku-ji (Silver Pavilion)
- Evening: Gion district — geisha district, walk Hanamikoji street at dusk (chance of seeing geiko/maiko heading to appointments)
- Dinner: Gion area izakaya or kaiseki

### Jun 5 (Fri) · West Kyoto · Arashiyama + Golden Pavilion
- Early morning: Arashiyama bamboo grove — go at 7–8am to beat crowds
- Tenryu-ji Zen garden + Togetsukyo bridge
- Optional: monkey park (hike up for views of Kyoto)
- Lunch: Arashiyama (yudofu — hot tofu, Kyoto specialty)
- Afternoon: Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) on the way back toward city
- Evening: relaxed dinner downtown, last night in Kyoto — sake bar crawl

### Jun 6 (Sat) · Travel to Hakone
- Morning: check out, Shinkansen Kyoto → Odawara (~2h)
- Local train/bus into Hakone, ryokan check-in afternoon

## Tokyo — June 7–14

### Jun 7 (Sun) · Arrival + Shinjuku
- Afternoon: Romancecar train Hakone → Shinjuku (~1.5h, scenic, book assigned seats)
- Check in, settle
- Sunset: **Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building** — free observation deck (45F), Fuji views on clear days, open till ~22:00
- Dusk: "Tokyo Night & Light" projection mapping show on the TMG exterior
- Evening: Omoide Yokocho ("Memory Lane") — tiny yakitori stalls, smoky old-Tokyo atmosphere
- Night: **Golden Gai** — ~200 tiny themed bars in an alleyway, each fits 6-8 people (perfect for first night)

### Jun 8 (Mon) · Akihabara · Electric Town
- Full day in Akihabara — anime, manga, figures, electronics
- Super Potato — legendary retro game shop, multiple floors of rare consoles and games
- Maid café visit (once, for the experience)
- Game center crawl — Taito Station, Sega arcade, crane games
- Evening: Akihabara stays lit up late, or back to Shinjuku
- Night: **8bit Café** (Shinjuku) — retro gaming bar, NES/SNES/arcade consoles, cocktails

### Jun 9 (Tue) · Harajuku + Shibuya
- Morning: Meiji Shrine — forest in the middle of Tokyo, calm break
- Takeshita Street — Harajuku youth culture, cosplay, crepes, kawaii shops
- Omotesando walk — tree-lined, upscale
- Afternoon: Shibuya Crossing, Shibuya Sky (observation deck with 360° views)
- Shopping in Shibuya
- Evening: **Round One Shibuya** — multiple floors of arcade, crane games, sports, bowling
- Night: Shibuya clubs — Womb (techno legend), Sound Museum Vision, or Contact

### Jun 10 (Wed) · Ghibli + Nakano + Ikebukuro · Otaku Day 2
- 10:00–13:00: **Ghibli Museum** (Mitaka) — book the 10:00 entry slot in advance (May 10 release); Catbus, original short film, Totoro in the lobby
- 13:00–13:30: Chuo Line east → Nakano (1 stop, ~5 min)
- 13:30–15:30: **Nakano Broadway** — 4-floor mall, retro games, rare figures, less touristy than Akihabara (grab lunch here too)
- 16:00–19:00: **Ikebukuro** — Animate HQ (largest anime store in Japan), Sunshine City, Pokemon Center
- 19:00–21:00: Ikebukuro izakayas — dinner + drinks
- 21:00+: **Karaoke Kan** or **Big Echo** — private rooms, all-night plans available

### Jun 11 (Thu) · Asakusa + Odaiba
- Morning: Asakusa — Senso-ji temple, Nakamise shopping street, old Tokyo vibe
- Lunch: Tsukiji outer market — sushi, street food
- Afternoon: Odaiba (futuristic man-made island)
  - Life-size Gundam statue
  - **teamLab Borderless** — immersive digital art (book tickets in advance)
  - Joypolis — indoor Sega theme park
- Evening: back to center, relaxed dinner

### Jun 12 (Fri) · Indie Tokyo + Tokyo Tower + Roppongi
- Morning/afternoon: Shimokitazawa — vintage shops, indie music, coffee, bookshops (Tokyo's counterculture neighborhood)
- Optional alternative: day trip to Kamakura (1h train) — Great Buddha, coastal temples
- **Late afternoon / golden hour — Tokyo Tower walk (sequence from north to south):**
  - **Zojoji Temple** — massive Sangedatsumon gate frames Tokyo Tower behind it (iconic photo); rows of Jizo statues with pinwheels
  - **Tokyo Tower** — walk over, photos from the base (not going up); stays till dusk for the light-up
  - **Akabanebashi 7-Eleven** — the viral spot south of the tower; exit the store and Tokyo Tower is framed perfectly between buildings
- Evening: Roppongi — international nightlife, bars, clubs, more expats/English-speakers (best night for meeting internationals)
- Options: Bar 16 Shots (arcade bar), rooftop bars, club crawl

### Jun 13 (Sat) · Imperial Palace → Ginza → Kabukicho finale
- **Morning: Imperial Palace East Gardens** (Kokyo-Higashi-Gyoen) — free public gardens inside the palace grounds; enter via Otemachi or Hirakawa gate; walk through the castle ruins, moats, and Ninomaru Garden (~1–1.5h). *Closed Mon/Fri — Jun 13 is Sat, all good.*
- **Tip:** grab a luxury bento from a **depachika** (Matsuya or Mitsukoshi basement) in Ginza first, picnic in the gardens
- Exit south → walk to **Tokyo Midtown Hibiya — Park View Garden** (6F rooftop, free) — greenery + views over Hibiya Park and toward the Palace
- Walk 5 min east into **Ginza**:
  - **Ginza Six** — upscale mall, free rooftop garden with city views
  - **Itoya** — 12-floor stationery flagship (paper, pens, notebooks)
  - **Uniqlo Ginza** — 12-floor global flagship, cheap UT collab tees as souvenirs
  - **Don Quijote Ginza** — last-minute snacks/souvenirs
  - **Kabukiza Theatre** — buy a **Hitomaku-mi** (single-act) ticket from the side entrance for ~1h of real Kabuki, cheap and very Japan-y
- Late afternoon / early evening: **Ginza Corridor-Gai** — izakayas + bars under the JR tracks
- Dinner: splurge — **wagyu** at a yakiniku grill, or a nice sushi counter (Ginza is *the* sushi neighborhood; lunch sets at top counters are more affordable than dinner if you want to try one)
- Night: back to Shinjuku → **Kabukicho** bar hop, karaoke finale, clubs

### Jun 14 (Sun) · Departure
- Check out, train to Narita or Haneda airport
- Fly home
